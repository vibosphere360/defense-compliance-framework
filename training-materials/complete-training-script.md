## MODULE 1: THE THREE AGENCIES (Slides 1.1-1.4)

### Slide 1.1: Who Controls What?

**Text:**
> ### Three Agencies. Three Different Jobs.
>
> | Agency | Controls | Watch Phrase |
> |--------|----------|---------------|
> | **DDTC** (State) | Military items (USML) | "If it kills people, DDTC rules" |
> | **BIS** (Commerce) | Dual-use items (CCL) | "If it has a civilian use, BIS rules" |
> | **OFAC** (Treasury) | Sanctions | "If the country is banned, OFAC rules" |

**Presenter Script:**

"Three agencies control exports from the United States.

First, DDTC – the State Department. They control military items. Weapons, drones, night vision. My watch phrase: 'If it kills people, DDTC rules.' That's ITAR.

Second, BIS – the Commerce Department. They control dual-use items – things with civilian and military applications. 'If it has a civilian use, BIS rules.' That's EAR.

Third, OFAC – the Treasury Department. They don't care what the item is. They care who you're sending it to. Sanctioned countries, terrorists, drug traffickers. 'If the country is banned, OFAC rules.'"

### Slide 2.2: The Deemed Export (Most Important Slide)

**Text:**
> ### What Is a "Deemed Export"?
>
> *"When you show or tell ITAR-controlled technical data to a foreign national – even if you never leave your desk – the law treats that as an export to their home country."*
>
> ### Real Example
>
> | Step | Action |
> |------|--------|
> | 1 | You are in San Francisco |
> | 2 | A French engineer joins your team |
> | 3 | You show them a CAD drawing of a drone part |
> | 4 | ✅ That is a "deemed export" to France |
> | 5 | ✅ You need a license BEFORE sharing |

> *"Your desk is now the border."*

**Presenter Script:**

"This is the most important slide in this training.

A deemed export happens when you show ITAR data to a foreign national. Your desk is the border. You need a license before that sharing happens.

This is the number one violation we see. Engineers sharing screenshots, CAD files, or code with foreign national teammates without realizing it's an export.

If you are not sure whether someone is a U.S. person, ask compliance before you share. Not after. Before."

### Slide 2.4: ITAR Data IS CUI

**Text:**
> ### ITAR/EAR Data = CUI
>
> *"All ITAR and EAR-controlled technical data is Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)."*
>
> ### What That Means For You
>
> | Requirement | Action |
> |-------------|--------|
> | **Marking** | `CUI//ITAR` or `CUI//EAR` on every page, top and bottom |
> | **Protection** | Follow NIST 800-171 controls |
> | **CMMC** | If you touch ITAR, you are in scope for CMMC |

> *"No marking = no protection = violation."*

**Presenter Script:**

"All ITAR and EAR technical data is CUI. That means three things.

First, marking. Every document, every email, every CAD file must be marked `CUI//ITAR` or `CUI//EAR` at the top and bottom of every page.

Second, protection. CUI requires NIST 800-171 controls – encryption, access control, logging.

Third, CMMC. If you touch ITAR data, your system is in scope for CMMC certification.

No marking equals no protection equals a violation."

### Slide 3.5: The Asylum Status Discussion

**Text:**
> ### U.S. Person Status: Legal vs. Operational
>
> #### Legal Baseline (ITAR 22 CFR §120.15)
> | Status | ITAR Says |
> |--------|-----------|
> | U.S. Citizen | ✅ U.S. Person |
> | Green Card | ✅ U.S. Person |
> | Asylee/Refugee | ✅ U.S. Person *legally* |
>
> #### Operational Reality
> | Risk Factor | Explanation |
> |-------------|-------------|
> | ⚠️ Provisional status | Asylum can be denied after access granted |
> | ⚠️ No automated revocation | USCIS doesn't notify HR in real time |
> | ⚠️ Insider risk window | Access persists until manually revoked |

> *"Legal minimums are not security best practices."*

**Presenter Script:**

"This is a nuanced topic. Under ITAR, asylees are legally U.S. persons. That's the law.

However, asylum status is provisional. It can be denied. When that happens, USCIS does not automatically notify our HR system. Access could persist for days or weeks after someone is no longer legally authorized.

That's a gap. Until we have real-time status verification, we apply a risk-based standard. Legal asylees get conditional access with enhanced monitoring."

## MODULE 7: CUI DISPOSAL & ITAD (Slides 7.1-7.2)

### Slide 7.1: NIST SP 800-88 – Clear, Purge, Destroy

**Text:**
> ### Three Disposal Levels (NIST SP 800-88)
>
> | Level | Method | When to Use |
> |-------|--------|--------------|
> | **Clear** | Overwrite with random data (1 pass HDD, block erase SSD) | REUSING media inside organization |
> | **Purge** | Degauss HDD, cryptographic erase SSD | GIVING media to another organization |
> | **Destroy** | Shred, incinerate, melt | DISPOSING permanently |

> *"Delete is not destroy. If you can't prove it was destroyed, assume it wasn't."*

**Presenter Script:**

"Delete is not destroy. Clear – overwrite. For reuse inside your company. Purge – degauss for HDDs, cryptographic erase for SSDs. For giving to another organization. Destroy – shred or incinerate. For permanent disposal.

Always get a Certificate of Destruction. Serial number, method, date, signature. Retain for 5 years."
